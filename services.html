Services

Six ways TBA helps organizations improve.

Every engagement starts with understanding what's actually happening. From there, we design the right approach — whether that's an assessment, a consulting engagement, engagement design, training, coaching, or facilitation.

01
Assessment

Understand your organization's current state.

You know your organization best. You and your leadership team are the most qualified people to assess the health and fitness of your business. What you need is a framework to facilitate the assessment process.

Our Organizational Self-Assessment provides a safe, effective, and expertly facilitated approach that helps you make better decisions about whether and how your organization should improve.

Clear view of where work is getting stuck
Root causes behind delays and pressure
A practical, prioritized improvement plan
Start a conversation
02
Consulting

Expert guidance for improving your organization.

Every organization can benefit from the perspective of well-intentioned and capable external support. We help you explore possibilities, identify opportunities, select the best options, and design the best approach for your next steps.

We don't arrive with a pre-packaged solution. We start by understanding what's actually happening — and build from there.

Rapid diagnosis of structural bottlenecks
Clear, prioritized improvement options
Practical implementation support
Start a conversation
03
Engagement Design

Design the right engagement for your context.

A successful organizational assessment uncovers options for improvement. For those options to become meaningful change, a framework is needed to guide the selection, design, and implementation of change initiatives.

Together, we apply the principles of evolutionary change management so that the sustainable growth of your organization has the best chance to succeed and thrive.

Change initiatives matched to organizational readiness
Systematic approach to sustainable improvement
Built around your actual operating conditions
Start a conversation
04
Training

Build the capability to sustain improvement.

World-class, evidence-based training for leaders, managers, and teams — delivered in classroom and virtual formats. Practical, pragmatic, and designed to create measurable change in how people work.

If you are serious about developing along a leadership path or developing leaders in your organization, there is substantial, measurable benefit from these courses.

Lean & Agile methodology grounded in practice
Classroom and virtual delivery
Actionable from day one
Start a conversation
05
Coaching

Close accompaniment during application.

Training and consulting give you the framework and the plan. Coaching is what happens when you start applying them — and the real challenges emerge.

Offered to executives and managers, coaching provides close accompaniment in the early stages of putting new approaches into practice. It is not instruction. It is presence alongside the work — a sounding board for reflection, a source of encouragement when unexpected obstacles appear, and an experienced mentor available when alignment and focus are needed most.

Greater confidence in capacities being built
Consistent guidance to maintain alignment and focus during application
Support in building new habits and leaving behind dysfunctional ones
Start a conversation
06
Facilitation

Structured facilitation for better meetings and workshops.

For leaders who want support facilitating and leading meetings and workshops with multiple collaborators. This is not consulting or coaching — it is focused, expert facilitation of pre-planned, scheduled sessions.

With some light preparation for context, Travis designs and leads sessions with clearly defined agendas, inputs, and outputs — ensuring that participants leave aligned and ready to act, not just informed.

The Leadership Continuity Lab is a structured example of this approach applied to leadership teams over an 8-week period.

More structured workshops and meetings with clear agendas
Defined inputs and outputs so participants know what to bring and what they'll leave with
Alignment and action-oriented outcomes rather than open-ended discussion
Start a conversation
